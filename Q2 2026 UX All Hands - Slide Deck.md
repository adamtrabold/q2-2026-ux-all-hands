## Design Notes

**S.T.A.R. Moment:** Slide 13 — "We can FINALLY cook!" with visceral chef photograph. This is the emotional peak and most memorable visual.

**Visual Motifs Used:**
- **STRUCTURE diagrams:** Showing frameworks (skeptic/debunker, team roles, accountability layers)
- **FLOW diagrams:** Showing progressions (tool evolution, automation filtering, deep knowledge → products)
- **INFLUENCE diagrams:** Showing cause/effect (skeptic inquiry, signal/noise, assistance radiating)
- **CLUSTER diagrams:** Showing groupings (automation vs assistance)
- **RADIATE diagrams:** Showing hub-and-spoke (assistance amplifying expertise)

**Photography Approach:**
- Real environments, not staged corporate stock
- Rule of thirds composition throughout
- Hands/action shots to show making and mastery
- Natural/gritty textures over polished surfaces

**What Is vs. What Could Be Structure:**
- Slides 1-10: What Is (landscape changing, skeptic framework, new context)
- Slides 11-17: What Could Be (unchanged principles, "we can cook", automation/assistance framework)
- Slides 18-20: The Challenge (hard work, muscle building, making time)
- Slides 21-24: The Opportunity (makers, nimble, concrete next steps)

**Total Slides:** 26 virtual slides (24 physical slides, with framework slide counting as 3) for ~10 minute presentation = ~23 seconds per slide average, with natural variations for beats that need more/less time.

**Note:** Slides 5-7 are one physical slide with three column states that progressively reveal. Each column state registers as a separate slide in navigation (dots, URLs, slide counter), but the slide itself doesn't advance—only the active column changes opacity.
